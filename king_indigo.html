King Indigo

King Indigo is the legendary title granted to Tom Letanio after he became the savior of Hell. Before the Guardians, before the Zavila Galaxy, Tom’s story began in the infernal realm — where he rose from a simple worker to the most respected warrior Hell had ever seen.

1 — Before the Crown: Tom at I.M.P

Long before he was King Indigo, Tom Letanio worked at the I.M.P (Immediate Murder Professionals). At the time, no one — not even the I.M.P crew — knew the truth:

Tom was both a Stand User and an Aetherian.

He hid his powers, choosing to live quietly among Hell’s chaotic workforce. But destiny had other plans.

2 — The Savior of Hell

When Hell faced its greatest crisis — a demonic uprising fueled by corrupted Aether — Tom stepped forward. Using his Stand, Indigo Fist, and his Aether abilities, he fought through hordes of corrupted demons, saving thousands.

His bravery spread across every ring of Hell. Even the Overlords took notice.

Tom became known as: “The Indigo Flame that Hell could not extinguish.”

3 — Lucifer’s Recognition

After witnessing Tom’s unmatched strength and compassion, Lucifer Morningstar summoned him to the Celestial Throne of Hell.

In front of the royals, the Overlords, the I.M.P, and even the Hazbin Hotel residents, Lucifer declared:

“Tom Letanio — you are the savior of Hell. From this day forward, you shall be known as King Indigo.”

The entire realm bowed. For the first time in Hell’s history, a mortal‑born Aetherian was crowned.

4 — The Rise of King Indigo

As King Indigo, Tom became a symbol of hope — a warrior who protected Hell from corruption, rogue demons, and cosmic threats. His Stand and Aether abilities made him nearly unstoppable.

The I.M.P praised him. The Hazbin Hotel honored him. The Overlords respected him. The citizens adored him.

Tom Letanio became the hero Hell never expected — but desperately needed.

5 — Legacy

Even after leaving Hell and joining the Guardians, Tom carried the title of King Indigo with pride. His past shaped him — the battles, the crown, the people he saved.

To this day, Hell still sings of the warrior who rose from nothing and became their king.
